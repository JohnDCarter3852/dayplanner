Setup Guide

Is this a standalone app or web-based?
This is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — a web page that behaves like a native app when saved to your iPhone home screen. It is not downloaded from the App Store.

What it IS: A full-screen app that lives on your home screen, works offline for stored data, fires notifications, and remembers everything in your iPhone's local storage.

What it is NOT: It does not replace your lock screen. Your lock screen is controlled by iOS and cannot be replaced by a third-party app without jailbreaking. This app opens when you tap its icon, just like any other app.
✅ It looks and feels like a native app. No browser chrome, full screen, home screen icon.
1
Add to iPhone Home Screen
1. Open this file in Safari (not Chrome — must be Safari)
2. Tap the Share button (box with arrow pointing up)
3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen
4. Name it Day and tap Add
5. Find the icon on your home screen and open it

From now on it opens full-screen with no browser bar.
⚠️ Must be opened in Safari. Chrome on iPhone cannot add PWAs to the home screen.
2
Connect Microsoft 365 (Outlook Calendar + Email)
Register your app in Azure (one-time, ~5 minutes):
1. Go to portal.azure.com and sign in with your work account
2. Search for App registrations → click New registration
3. Name: anything (e.g. "My Day Planner")
4. Supported account types: Accounts in any organisational directory and personal Microsoft accounts
5. Redirect URI: choose Single-page application (SPA) → enter https://localhost
6. Click Register
7. Copy the Application (client) ID — paste it in ⚙ Setup
8. Go to API permissions → Add a permission → Microsoft Graph → Delegated
9. Add: Calendars.Read, Mail.Read, User.Read
10. Click Grant admin consent (or ask your IT admin)

Then in this app: tap ⚙ → paste Client ID → tap Sign in with Microsoft.
You'll sign in once and stay connected.
💡 Your IT admin may need to approve the consent step. Send them the app registration link and ask them to grant tenant-wide consent.
3
Connect Google Maps (Live Traffic Alerts)
1. Go to console.cloud.google.com
2. Create a new project (or use an existing one)
3. Go to APIs & Services → Library
4. Search for and enable: Distance Matrix API
5. Go to APIs & Services → Credentials
6. Click Create Credentials → API Key
7. Copy the key (starts with AIza…)
8. Paste it in ⚙ Setup → Google Maps API Key

Cost: Google gives you $200 free credit per month. For personal use this app will cost essentially nothing.
✅ Once connected, every meeting with a location gets a live traffic-adjusted "leave by" time and a notification.
4
WhatsApp / SMS — Callbacks & Messages
WhatsApp and iMessage do not allow third-party apps to read your messages directly — this is an iOS and WhatsApp security restriction that cannot be bypassed.

For WhatsApp:
1. Open the chat in WhatsApp
2. Tap the contact name at the top
3. Scroll down → Export Chat → Without Media
4. Copy all the text and paste it into the Callbacks tab

For iMessage / SMS:
1. Long-press any message → Copy
2. Or select multiple messages and copy the thread
3. Paste into the Callbacks tab — AI will extract missed calls and callback commitments

Voice shortcut: While using the app, tap the mic and say "I need to call [name] back" — it logs instantly without pasting anything.
⚠️ Apple and WhatsApp intentionally prevent any app from reading your private messages. Paste is the only safe, approved method.
5
Notifications, Alarms & Morning Routine
Allow notifications: When you first open the app it asks for notification permission — tap Allow. This enables meeting alerts and leave-time reminders.

Link to your morning alarm (Apple Shortcuts):
1. Open the Shortcuts app on your iPhone
2. Tap Automation → New Automation
3. Choose Alarm → select your morning alarm
4. Add action: Open App → choose Day (this app)
5. Turn off "Ask Before Running"
6. Tap Done

Now when your alarm goes off, this app opens automatically so your day is ready before you even get out of bed.
✅ Combine with "Generate My Day" to get your AI briefing the moment your alarm fires.
6
Does this replace my lock screen?
No — and this is intentional. Apple does not allow any app to replace the iOS lock screen. The lock screen is a core security feature controlled entirely by Apple.

What you can do instead:
• Add a Lock Screen widget in iOS (Settings → Wallpaper → Customise Lock Screen → Widgets) showing your calendar or reminders
• Set this app to open automatically when your alarm fires using Shortcuts (see Step 5)
• Add a Dynamic Island or notification that shows your next meeting

This means your lock screen stays secure while this app opens instantly the moment you pick up your phone.
⚠️ Any app claiming to "replace" the iOS lock screen requires jailbreaking, which voids your warranty and creates security risks.
